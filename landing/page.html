How it unfolds

Three small things. The rest becomes your radio.

Nothing here asks for your attention. The ecosystem keeps its own time, even while the app is closed.

01 — Plant

Choose a land, plant a little

Pick Kyoto or Charlotte, each with its own climate and birds. Plants call insects; plants and insects together call the birds.

02 — Step away

Let time pass

Close the app and live your day. The food web keeps moving. When you come back, a new bird may have arrived while you were gone.

03 — Listen

Your radio grows

Every bird you meet joins your garden radio — real recordings singing back and forth over soft, healing sound. The more birds you collect, the fuller it plays. It never shrinks.

The cast

A little chorus that grows as you listen

Every bird you meet is drawn in its own chunky pixel form and kept in your field guide — always there to visit, even after it leaves the garden. New species are being added all the time, so the cast keeps growing.

Quiet promises

The things we will never do to you

Toris Collection is built on a few rules we won't break, whatever else changes.

We never rush you

No stamina, no timers, no daily obligations. Come and go as you like — the garden waits without guilt.

Nothing punishes you

A storm may thin your garden, but your collection never shrinks. The birds you've met, and the days you've spent together, are kept.

Your radio is always free

Listening and calm are never behind a paywall. Paid extras are only color and bridges to the real world — never the sound itself.

True to real ecology

Which bird comes, and why, follows a real food web built from open interaction data. No invented reasons.

Cuteness, first

The moment a bird arrives, its call, its pixel art — protected above everything else.

Made of real things

Real songs, a real web of life

Real recordings

The calls you hear are field recordings from xeno-canto, a community archive of wild bird sound from around the world.

Real relationships

Who eats what, who follows whom — the food web is seeded from GloBI (Global Biotic Interactions), built on real records.
